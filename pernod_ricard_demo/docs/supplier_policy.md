# Pernod Ricard Japan - Supplier Management Policy

## 1. Supplier Qualification Requirements

### 1.1 Mandatory Certifications
All suppliers providing materials that come into contact with food or beverages MUST hold at least one of the following certifications:
- **ISO 22000** (Food Safety Management)
- **FSSC 22000** (Food Safety System Certification)
- **HACCP** (Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points)

Additionally, all suppliers must hold:
- **ISO 9001** (Quality Management System)

### 1.2 Preferred Certifications
The following certifications are preferred and contribute to higher supplier scores:
- **ISO 14001** (Environmental Management)
- **JAS** (Japanese Agricultural Standards)
- **Organic JAS** (有機JAS)

## 2. Supplier Scoring Criteria

| Criteria | Weight | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Quality Score | 30% | Based on quality inspection pass rate |
| Delivery Reliability | 25% | On-time delivery percentage |
| Price Competitiveness | 20% | Compared to market benchmarks |
| Certification Level | 15% | Number and relevance of certifications |
| Sustainability | 10% | Environmental and social responsibility |

### 2.1 Minimum Acceptable Scores
- **Overall Score**: Minimum 0.65 (65%) to remain an active supplier
- **Quality Score**: Minimum 0.70 (70%) - non-negotiable
- **Delivery Reliability**: Minimum 0.60 (60%)

## 3. Audit Requirements

### 3.1 Audit Frequency
- **Tier 1 Suppliers** (critical materials): Annual audit required
- **Tier 2 Suppliers** (packaging, logistics): Audit every 18 months
- **Tier 3 Suppliers** (indirect materials): Audit every 24 months

### 3.2 Audit Expiry
- Suppliers with last audit date older than **12 months** for Tier 1, **18 months** for Tier 2, or **24 months** for Tier 3 must be flagged for re-audit
- Suppliers with no audit date on record must be immediately escalated

## 4. Pricing Guidelines

### 4.1 Acceptable Price Ranges (JPY)
| Category | Min Unit Price | Max Unit Price |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Glass Bottles | 80 | 5,000 |
| Packaging | 30 | 3,000 |
| Labels & Printing | 10 | 1,500 |
| Corks & Caps | 20 | 2,000 |
| Raw Materials | 100 | 50,000 |
| Logistics & Transport | 500 | 100,000 |
| Quality Inspection | 1,000 | 200,000 |
| Brewing Equipment | 5,000 | 500,000 |

### 4.2 Anomaly Threshold
Any unit price exceeding **3x the category maximum** must be flagged and escalated for human review.

## 5. Lead Time Standards

### 5.1 Maximum Acceptable Lead Times (Days)
| Category | Standard | Express |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Glass Bottles | 45 | 14 |
| Packaging | 30 | 7 |
| Labels & Printing | 21 | 5 |
| Corks & Caps | 30 | 10 |
| Raw Materials | 60 | 21 |
| Logistics & Transport | 14 | 3 |

### 5.2 Lead Time Anomaly
Any lead time exceeding **2x the standard maximum** for its category must be flagged.

## 6. Japan-Specific Compliance

### 6.1 Food Safety Act (食品衛生法)
All suppliers of materials contacting beverages must comply with Japan's Food Safety Act and hold appropriate registrations with local health centers (保健所).

### 6.2 Liquor Tax Act (酒税法)
Suppliers involved in alcohol production materials must be registered under the Liquor Tax Act.

### 6.3 Container & Packaging Recycling Act
All packaging suppliers must comply with Japan's recycling regulations and provide recyclability documentation.

## 7. Duplicate Supplier Policy
- Duplicate supplier records must be merged into a single "golden record"
- The golden record retains the most complete and recent information
- All historical transaction data from duplicate records must be linked to the golden record
- Duplicate detection should consider: company name variations (JP/EN), address similarity, phone number matching
